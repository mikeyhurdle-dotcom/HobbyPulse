If you only need one sentence: **buy for your group’s table reality, not the Hotness rank.**

BGG’s April chart is full of temptation right now: **Lairs, Eternal Decks, Bailiff of Boscoop, Aygor: Demons' Hollow, LOTR: Fate of the Fellowship, Nippon: Zaibatsu**.

Some of these are great buys. Some are absolutely the wrong fit unless your group already likes that weight and cadence.

## Fast picks: Buy / Wait / Skip

- **Buy now:** Lairs, The Lord of the Rings: Fate of the Fellowship, Nippon: Zaibatsu (for the right group)
- **Wait for more data:** Aygor: Demons' Hollow, Bailiff of Boscoop
- **Skip for most casual groups:** Eternal Decks (unless your table actively wants puzzle pressure)

## Why this month is noisy

Three trend signals are colliding:

1. New-release visibility from BGG Hotness
2. Creator coverage spikes around tactical and campaign-heavy titles
3. FOMO buying from “it’s trending, so it must fit my group” logic

That third one is where money gets wasted.

## 1) Lairs — BUY for tactical groups, skip for low-friction game nights

**Why it’s trending:** fast buzz, strong identity, easy to explain in one line.

### Best for
- Groups that enjoy direct tactical interaction
- Players happy with a bit of mean play and adaptation

### Skip if
- Your group prefers low-conflict engines or cosy tableau builders
- You only get one play a month and need instant smoothness

### Trade-off
Lairs gives high interaction and table energy, but that same interaction can punish less experienced players hard in early plays.

**Who should skip:** new mixed-skill groups where one or two players dislike being targeted.

## 2) Eternal Decks — WAIT unless your group likes puzzle pressure

I like what Eternal Decks is trying to do, but this is where Hotness can mislead buyers.

### Best for
- Players who enjoy efficiency puzzles and failure-retry loops
- Tables that like discussing optimal lines between rounds

### Skip if
- Your group wants social, talky, loose game nights
- AP (analysis paralysis) already slows your sessions

### Trade-off
Great puzzle depth, but pacing can drag if even one player overthinks each stage.

**Who should skip:** groups already struggling to finish medium-weight games in one sitting.

## 3) The Lord of the Rings: Fate of the Fellowship — BUY for theme-first co-op tables

This one is converting because theme + accessibility is a reliable combo.

### Best for
- Co-op groups with mixed skill levels
- Theme-first players who want a story arc without a brutal rules burden

### Skip if
- Your group wants hard, deterministic optimization over narrative moments

### Trade-off
Strong accessibility and atmosphere, but lower replay novelty for groups that prioritize mechanical variety over theme.

**Who should skip:** players who treat every purchase as a long-term competitive puzzle box.

## 4) Nippon: Zaibatsu — BUY if you already know your group likes heavier euros

Nippon keeps momentum because it offers meaningful decisions every round.

### Best for
- Dedicated strategy groups
- Players who are happy with heavier teach overhead in exchange for depth

### Skip if
- Your table rarely commits to longer sessions
- You’re buying for a broad family/casual audience

### Trade-off
High decision quality and replay value, but onboarding friction is real.

**Who should skip:** anyone hoping this becomes a “pull out any night” game.

## A concrete buying scenario (where people get this wrong)

You have a four-player group:
- two experienced euro players,
- one casual player,
- one player who dislikes direct pressure.

If you buy purely from Hotness rank, you’ll probably over-index on weight or confrontation and table the game once.

If you buy for *actual table fit*, you likely choose one high-energy tactical game (**Lairs**) *or* one co-op accessibility win (**LOTR**), not both at once.

That’s a better use of budget and shelf space.

## Final recommendation

For April 2026, don’t chase the whole chart. Buy **one** game that clearly matches your group’s friction tolerance.

If your table is mixed experience, start with **Fate of the Fellowship**.
If your group likes pressure and tactical play, start with **Lairs**.
If your group is already euro-heavy and committed, **Nippon: Zaibatsu** is the best long-term value.

Everything else: wait for two more weeks of real player feedback before spending.
