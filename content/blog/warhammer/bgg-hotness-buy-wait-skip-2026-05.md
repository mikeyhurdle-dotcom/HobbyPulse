If you only have budget for one new box this month, don’t shop the Hotness list by rank alone. Rank tracks attention, not fit. Below is the practical filter: who each game is for, where the risk sits, and who should skip.

## 1) Lands of Evershade — **Buy if your group commits to a campaign**

This is the one getting the biggest attention spike, and the appeal is obvious: campaign progression, tactical fights, and story hooks in one box.

**Trade-off / caveat:** campaign games die when attendance is inconsistent. If your table rotates players week to week, the experience degrades fast.

**Who should skip:** groups that mostly play one-shot sessions under 90 minutes.

## 2) World Order — **Wait unless your group likes negotiation-heavy conflict**

The theme and scope are strong, but area-control politics titles are group-dependent. Great at the right table; miserable at the wrong one.

**Trade-off / caveat:** high interpersonal friction by design. That’s a feature for some groups and a hard stop for others.

**Who should skip:** players who dislike table-talk pressure, temporary alliances, or kingmaking endgames.

## 3) Inkwell — **Buy for medium-weight strategy nights**

Inkwell is trending because it looks approachable while still offering meaningful decisions. It’s the safest “new-hotness” pick for mixed-skill groups.

**Trade-off / caveat:** if your group prefers deeply asymmetric or highly interactive systems, this can feel too clean and puzzle-forward.

**Who should skip:** groups that want loud conflict and chaos every session.

## 4) Nippon: Zaibatsu — **Buy if you already know you like economic optimization**

This is not a casual pickup. It rewards planning, timing, and efficiency play over spectacle.

**Trade-off / caveat:** teach overhead and cognitive load are real. First play can feel opaque without a strong teacher.

**Who should skip:** new hobby gamers still building confidence with worker-placement/economy systems.

## 5) Heat: Pedal to the Metal — **Buy for reliable table turnout**

Not new, but still trending for a reason: it lands with casual and core players alike and gets repeat plays.

**Trade-off / caveat:** long-term depth depends on your appetite for modules/variants and group familiarity.

**Who should skip:** players who only want heavy euro crunch and minimal luck swings.

## Three currently hot titles I’d **wait** on

### Dark Pact
Looks promising, but market heat is running ahead of broad table data. Wait for more session reports and post-hype rule clarifications.

### Eternal Decks
If your local group doesn’t already support the deck-construction style this uses, you risk buying into a game that never hits the table.

### Flockers
Strong discovery buzz, but limited data on replay arc and whether the novelty sustains beyond first impressions.

## Quick shortlist by table type

- **Busy group, low prep:** Heat: Pedal to the Metal  
- **Campaign-committed group:** Lands of Evershade  
- **Medium-weight strategy mix:** Inkwell  
- **Hardcore optimization table:** Nippon: Zaibatsu  
- **Negotiation-first group:** World Order (after a expectations check)

Hotness is useful for discovery, not final purchase decisions. Use it to build a shortlist, then buy the game your table will actually replay in six weeks.
