## The Contenders
Both games sit in the same strategic family, but they reward slightly different priorities.

- **Brass: Birmingham** is the established benchmark: tight economic timing, punishing efficiency checks, and a huge competitive scene.
- **Brass: Pittsburgh** (newer entry) keeps the core network-and-industry DNA but shifts pacing and incentives enough to feel distinct rather than just a reskin.

If your group only wants one, this is mostly a question of *teach friction vs novelty payoff*.

## Which One Should You Buy?
### Buy **Brass: Birmingham** if…
- You want the proven “all-time heavy euro” pick.
- Your group likes mastering one game over many sessions.
- You prefer deeply studied openings and long-term planning.

### Buy **Brass: Pittsburgh** if…
- You want Brass tension with fresher puzzle texture.
- Your group dislikes feeling locked into one opening script.
- You value slightly faster table tempo in mixed-experience groups.

## What Doesn’t Translate from Hype
“Highest ranked” does not mean “best for your table.” Birmingham often wins raw prestige, but if your group stalls under heavy planning windows, Pittsburgh can hit the table more often—and that usually matters more than leaderboard status.

## Who Should Skip Each
- **Skip Birmingham** if your table regularly bounces off games above 2-hour first plays.
- **Skip Pittsburgh** if you specifically want the most widely discussed and tournament-tested Brass experience.

## Final Recommendation
If this is your **first Brass**, start with **Brass: Birmingham** unless your group strongly prefers tactical flexibility over rigid optimization.

If you already own Birmingham and want a second heavy economic title that feels related but not redundant, **Brass: Pittsburgh** is a strong follow-on purchase.
