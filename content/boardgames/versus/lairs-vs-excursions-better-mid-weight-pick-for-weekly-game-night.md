## Meta Description
Trying to choose between Lairs and Excursions? Compare teachability, player count fit, replay depth, and table feel to buy the right mid-weight game for weekly game night.

## At a Glance
- **Players:** 2-5

## The Short Answer
If your table wants smoother onboarding and faster setup, **Excursions** is usually the safer buy.

If your group likes slightly crunchier turns and stronger tactical interaction, **Lairs** has the better long-term upside.

## Head-to-Head
| Factor | Lairs | Excursions |
|---|---|---|
| Learning Curve | Moderate; iconography takes 1-2 rounds to click | Easier first teach and cleaner early turns |
| Table Interaction | Higher direct pressure and disruption moments | More parallel planning with lighter conflict |
| Session Pace | Can slow at 4-5 with analysis-heavy players | More consistent tempo across mixed groups |
| Replay Value | Better for groups that like tactical adaptation | Better for groups that rotate casual players |
| Best Player Count | 3-4 | 2-4 |
| Typical UK Price | £35-£55 | £30-£50 |

## Buy **Lairs** if...
- Your group enjoys tactical friction and reading opponents.
- You want a mid-weight game that still feels competitive after 10+ plays.
- Your regular table is mostly the same players week to week.

## Buy **Excursions** if...
- You often teach one or two new players at game night.
- You need a reliable 60-minute option that still has meaningful choices.
- Your table values low rules overhead and quick setup.

## What the Hype Doesn’t Tell You
Both games are often discussed as “easy recommendations,” but they solve different problems:

- **Lairs** is better when your group wants a little edge and table tension.
- **Excursions** is better when your group needs consistency and low friction.

If your shelf already has lots of low-conflict efficiency games, Lairs adds more contrast. If your shelf is full of brain-burners, Excursions will likely get played more often.

## Who Should Skip Each
- **Skip Lairs** if your table dislikes disruption or long think turns.
- **Skip Excursions** if your group is already asking for “something meatier” each week.

## Final Recommendation
For most mixed weekly game-night groups, **Excursions** is the higher-confidence first purchase.

For established hobby tables that meet regularly and want deeper tactical interaction, **Lairs** is the better long-term buy.
