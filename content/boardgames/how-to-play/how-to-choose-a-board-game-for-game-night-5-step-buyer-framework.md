## At a Glance
- **Players:** 2-8
- **Play time:** 20-180 minutes
- **Age:** 10+
- **Complexity:** Light to heavy
- **Price range:** £15-£90

## Step 1: Lock Your Real Player Count
Buy for your **most common** group size, not occasional max size. A brilliant 5-player game is a bad purchase if you usually play at 2.

## Step 2: Match Complexity to Energy
If your group meets after work, cap complexity around medium. Heavy games are excellent, but they fail when everyone is tired and rules patience is low.

## Step 3: Check Downtime Tolerance
Some groups love planning windows; others disengage after 90 seconds off-turn. Buy games with turn pace that fits your group’s attention span.

## Step 4: Audit Setup + Teach Friction
A 20-minute setup kills weeknight momentum. If you play often but briefly, prioritise games with fast setup and clear first turns.

## Step 5: Buy for Replay Pattern, Not Hype
Pick games with decision variety your group actually enjoys: tactical conflict, engine building, co-op puzzles, or social play.

## Common Mistakes
- Buying by ranking alone
- Ignoring who in your group dislikes conflict
- Paying launch prices before buyer reviews settle

## Trade-Off/Caveat
The big trade-off is **depth vs accessibility**. Deeper games can become favourites, but only if your group survives the first two plays.

## Who Should Skip This Framework
Skip this if you only buy collector editions regardless of table fit. This framework is for practical players trying to maximise play, not shelf prestige.

## Recommendation Fit
- **Best for:** Buyers who want practical table-fit guidance before spending.
