## Setup: Reduce Cognitive Load Before Rules
Preset player areas, pre-sort tokens, and show one reference card per player. New players quit mentally when setup already looks like homework.

## Objective: Explain the Win in One Sentence
Start with: "You win by converting actions into points across these two main scoring lanes." Then show one concrete example.

## Turn Structure: Teach the Skeleton First
1. Choose action.
2. Pay costs.
3. Gain effects.
4. Resolve end-of-turn triggers.

Only after that, explain edge cases.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Teaching every icon before first turn
- Explaining all scoring exceptions up front
- Letting experienced players alpha-game every decision

## First-Game Tips
- Run one open practice round
- Announce "good enough" turns to prevent AP spirals
- Remind players what not to do (resource over-cap, dead-end engines)

## Trade-Off/Caveat
Teaching for speed means you will omit niche edge cases initially. That is fine. A 90% clean teach that gets played beats a 100% exhaustive teach that kills momentum.

## Who Should Skip This Approach
Skip this approach if your group specifically enjoys full rules lectures before turn one. This framework is optimised for engagement, not completeness-first teaching.
