# TabletopWatch Top 50 — 2026 Q2

*By Quill. Updated 2026-04-30.*

This isn't BGG's top 50 with the serial numbers filed off, and it isn't an SU&SD greatest-hits run either. It's the list for the reader I keep in mind: 10–40 games on the Kallax already, a Wingspan or an Ark Nova in there, a partner who'll play 30-minute games but not three-hour ones, a regular group that's mixed-skill and mixed-attention. BGG's top 100 is over-indexed on heavy euros nine in ten readers play twice and shelve. Tom Vasel rates by enthusiasm, Quinns by argument. I rate by what stays on the table after two years — what gets pulled out when nobody's writing a review and nobody's filming a thumbnail.

Ranking is by "if I could only own this many games, what stays on the shelf". The top 10 mixes weight and length on purpose — gateway through heavy through party through solo — because a real game group needs the spread, not five identical mid-weight euros. There's at least five 2024-or-later picks in the top 30 because this list refreshes quarterly and currency matters. A few of these will annoy the BGG forum; I've kept the case to one sentence per pick. Prices are UK RRP, and where a game is properly hard to find UK-side I've said so. Skip what you've already got. Buy second-hand where it makes sense. Don't buy three games in one go — your shelf doesn't need it and your group will only play one.

## The Top 10

### 1. Cascadia (1-4 players, 30-45 min, £40 RRP, BGG weight 1.85)
Best gateway-plus on the market, the seven-year-old plays it, the Wednesday group plays it, the box still looks new after three years, that's the test most reviews skip.

### 2. Brass: Birmingham (2-4 players, 120-180 min, £75 RRP, BGG weight 3.97)
BGG #1 deserves the slot but only if you've got the table and the patience for a 30-minute teach, skip if anyone's promised they'll "pick it up as we go".

### 3. Wingspan (1-5 players, 60-90 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 2.46)
Beth Sobel's bird art and a clean engine that four players solve in parallel rather than against each other, fine, not transcendent, but the right next-step from Splendor for half the audience.

### 4. Spirit Island (1-4 players, 90-120 min, £85 RRP, BGG weight 4.04)
Best co-op I've taught and the one I'd recommend to maybe one in three groups who ask, the rules text on the powers is dense and unforgiving, the upgrade from Pandemic if Pandemic felt shallow.

### 5. Heat: Pedal to the Metal (1-6 players, 30-60 min, £45 RRP, BGG weight 2.07)
The 2022 release that finally cracked "racing as a real board game", plays clean at six, plays sharp at three, the expansion is genuinely worth the £30 not just the box-fattener most are.

### 6. Ark Nova (1-4 players, 90-150 min, £70 RRP, BGG weight 3.79)
Heavy zoo-builder, plays best at three, the card hand-management is what keeps it on the shelf, the puzzle is solvable enough that play six feels like play three which is the honest flaw nobody mentions.

### 7. Just One (3-7 players, 20 min, £20 RRP, BGG weight 1.04)
The party game that survived the room, including the relatives who normally won't play anything, you don't need another party game once this is in the cupboard.

### 8. The Crew: Mission Deep Sea (3-5 players, 20 min, £14 RRP, BGG weight 1.97)
Best cooperative trick-taker I've taught a non-trick-taking table, the £14 price tag is a gift, get this before any £55 deluxe co-op you're considering as your group co-op.

### 9. Daybreak (1-4 players, 60-90 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 2.74)
Matt Leacock's climate co-op, the 2023 release that does something Pandemic does not, which is make the cards on the table feel like real policy levers, skip if anyone at your table can't separate the politics from the play.

### 10. Sky Team (2 players, 15-20 min, £25 RRP, BGG weight 1.79)
2024 Spiel des Jahres winner, the only two-player I'd put in a top 10 right now, 15 minutes per game, the cockpit conceit is the rare theme that earns its keep, get this before Patchwork if you don't already own one.

## 11–25

### 11. Heat expansion: Heavy Rain / Tunnels (1-6 players, 30-60 min, £30 RRP, BGG weight 2.4)
Listed separately because it changes the base game enough to matter; the weather rules are the upgrade, ignore the legacy mode the first six plays.

### 12. Earth (1-5 players, 45-90 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 2.85)
Better tableau-builder than people give it credit for, plays in 60 minutes at three not the 90 the box claims, has comfortably replaced Wingspan in two of my groups.

### 13. Azul (2-4 players, 30-45 min, £32 RRP, BGG weight 1.77)
The one I've sold most copies of and the one that most reliably comes home with a smile, no apology for putting it at 13 not 3, the spatial scoring breaks for 10% of players and that's the honest range.

### 14. Splendor (2-4 players, 30 min, £35 RRP, BGG weight 1.80)
Eight years on it still teaches the engine-builder grammar in 30 minutes, skip if you already own Wingspan and Earth, you're past it.

### 15. Codenames (2-8 players, 15 min, £18 RRP, BGG weight 1.30)
The only word game in this top 25 because it's the only one that survives a mixed group without anyone feeling stupid.

### 16. Cosmic Encounter (3-5 players, 60-120 min, £45 RRP, BGG weight 2.74)
1977 design, every "asymmetric powers" game you've enjoyed since owes it royalties, plays rough at five and brilliantly at four, find the 42nd Anniversary edition not the 2008 one.

### 17. Ticket to Ride: Europe (2-5 players, 30-60 min, £45 RRP, BGG weight 1.91)
The base US game has aged worse than Europe, get Europe and skip the original, that's the only sensible call in 2026.

### 18. Lost Ruins of Arnak (1-4 players, 60-120 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 2.93)
Deck-building plus worker placement, the kind of mash-up that sounds tired but earns it, the Searchers expansion is the one to add not the Expedition Leaders.

### 19. Carnegie (1-4 players, 90-120 min, £65 RRP, BGG weight 3.45)
The 2022 Quined release that nobody seems to talk about and which is doing 80% of what Lisboa does at 60% of the brain-melt, properly hard to find UK-side, Zatu sometimes restocks.

### 20. SETI: Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (1-4 players, 120-180 min, £85 RRP, BGG weight 4.12)
2024 release, the heavy game I've put in front of three different groups this year that's actually held the table for play three and four, skip if you bounced off Ark Nova for being too solvable, this one's the same problem just bigger.

### 21. The Quiet Year (2-4 players, 90 min, £25 RRP, BGG weight 1.81)
Map-drawing storytelling game, half the table thinks it's brilliant and the other half thinks it's not a game, check who you're inviting before you put it on the table.

### 22. Patchwork (2 players, 15-30 min, £25 RRP, BGG weight 1.65)
Still the partner-game default for me, the one that pulls people in who otherwise won't sit down, beats Sky Team for sheer pickup-and-play but loses for theme.

### 23. Gloomhaven: Jaws of the Lion (1-4 players, 60-120 min, £50 RRP, BGG weight 3.71)
The right Gloomhaven for 95% of buyers, the original is for the obsessive 5%, JotL gets you 25 hours of campaign for £50 and a shelf-friendly box.

### 24. Cthulhu: Death May Die (1-5 players, 90 min, £75 RRP, BGG weight 2.37)
The dudes-on-a-map co-op that actually plays in 90 minutes not three hours, the dice are fixed enough that you'll feel like a hero on play one not play five.

### 25. The Castles of Burgundy: Special Edition (1-4 players, 60-90 min, £85 RRP, BGG weight 3.02)
2019 design that's now dressed up in a 2022 special edition, only buy the SE if you'll genuinely play 20+ times, otherwise the regular at £40 does the same job.

## 26–50

### 26. Concordia (2-5 players, 90-120 min, £50 RRP, BGG weight 3.10)
Still the cleanest mid-heavy euro of the last 15 years.

### 27. Race for the Galaxy (2-4 players, 30-60 min, £35 RRP, BGG weight 2.99)
2007 design, the icons take five plays to read fluently, after that it's brilliant.

### 28. Dune: Imperium (1-4 players, 60-120 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 3.03)
Best deck-builder + worker placement of the recent wave, Uprising is the standalone upgrade, skip Imperium if Uprising's available.

### 29. Forest Shuffle (2-4 players, 40-60 min, £30 RRP, BGG weight 2.16)
2023 Spiel nominee, plays Wingspan-shaped in half the time at half the price, properly underrated.

### 30. Faraway (2-6 players, 15-20 min, £22 RRP, BGG weight 1.84)
2024 release, my partner asks for this not the other way round, the timing-of-information twist is the actual game.

### 31. 7 Wonders Duel (2 players, 30 min, £30 RRP, BGG weight 2.23)
Best two-player I've owned that isn't Sky Team, get the Agora expansion only if you've played base 20 times.

### 32. The Castles of Tuscany (2-4 players, 45-60 min, £40 RRP, BGG weight 2.62)
Stefan Feld at his cleanest, plays Castles of Burgundy in 50 minutes which is the version a real table actually puts down.

### 33. Mind MGMT: The Psychic Espionage Operation (1-5 players, 60-90 min, £50 RRP, BGG weight 2.91)
Asymmetric one-vs-many hidden movement, the rules pamphlet is rough but the game underneath is the best Scotland Yard upgrade going.

### 34. Crokinole (2-4 players, 30 min, £140-£250, dexterity, no BGG weight)
If you have £140 and a flat table, this is the one game that earns itself back in nights.

### 35. Trickerion: Legends of Illusion (2-4 players, 90-150 min, £70 RRP, BGG weight 4.07)
Heavy euro about stage magicians, theme is doing 30% of the work and the work is real.

### 36. Cartographers Heroes (1-100 players, 30-45 min, £25 RRP, BGG weight 1.83)
Best flip-and-write going, plays solo, plays at six, plays in 30 minutes.

### 37. Sleeping Gods (1-4 players, 60+ min per session, £85 RRP, BGG weight 2.85)
Campaign storybook game that survives where Tainted Grail collapsed, the 2024 standalone Distant Skies is the better entry point now.

### 38. Marvel Champions: The Card Game (1-4 players, 45-90 min, £55 base + heroes at £15-£18, BGG weight 2.87)
The LCG that's actually sustainable, one hero pack is a campaign on its own.

### 39. Beyond the Sun (2-4 players, 60-120 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 3.46)
Tech tree game, the player-driven branching is the trick, never plays the same way twice and that's not the cliché it usually is.

### 40. Caverna: The Cave Farmers (1-7 players, 90-180 min, £75 RRP, BGG weight 3.74)
Agricola's friendlier sibling, the version of "farming euro" I'd actually keep on the shelf.

### 41. Disney Lorcana: starter set (2 players, 20-30 min, £18 starter, ongoing, BGG weight 2.60)
The trading card game my Wednesday group will actually play, not Magic, not Flesh and Blood, this one.

### 42. Mysterium Park (2-7 players, 30 min, £25 RRP, BGG weight 1.71)
The streamlined Mysterium that fixes the original's pacing, get Park not the original.

### 43. Hadrian's Wall (1-6 players, 60-90 min, £40 RRP, BGG weight 3.10)
Roll-and-write at full euro complexity, the 2021 release that proved roll-and-writes can be heavy.

### 44. Endeavor: Deep Sea (1-4 players, 60-90 min, £70 RRP, BGG weight 2.80)
2024 release, the redesign of Age of Sail without the colonial baggage, properly the better game now.

### 45. Welcome To... (1-100 players, 25 min, £30 RRP, BGG weight 1.84)
The flip-and-write that taught me the genre had legs.

### 46. Decrypto (3-8 players, 15-45 min, £20 RRP, BGG weight 1.83)
Codenames' clever sibling, harder to teach but better at its peak.

### 47. The Mind (2-4 players, 15-20 min, £10 RRP, BGG weight 1.18)
The £10 that earned itself back in the first session, do not overthink the buy.

### 48. Calico (1-4 players, 30-45 min, £35 RRP, BGG weight 2.59)
Tile-laying with cats, looks twee, plays sharp.

### 49. Caesar! Seize Rome in 20 Minutes! (2 players, 20 min, £35 RRP, BGG weight 2.10)
Two-player area control in 20 minutes, the Wallace design that gets less love than it should.

### 50. Imperial Steam (2-4 players, 90-120 min, £55 RRP, BGG weight 3.94)
2021 release, an 18xx-adjacent network builder for people who like the idea of 18xx but won't run a six-hour game, hard to find UK-side, check Spiele-Offensive.

## What's NOT on the list (and why)

### Twilight Imperium (4th Edition / Prophecy of Kings)
A six-hour game four times a year is a different hobby. If your table is the right table for it, you already own it; this list isn't for that table.

### Gloomhaven (original)
Jaws of the Lion is on the list at #23 because it's the right Gloomhaven for almost everyone. The original is a 95-hour campaign in a 10kg box. The shelf cost is the verdict.

### Catan
Catan is the gateway I cut my teeth on at uni. Cascadia, Ticket to Ride: Europe, and Azul are all the better first buy in 2026. Catan still sells; that doesn't make it the right pick.

### Pandemic Legacy: Season 1
A 12-session campaign that ends. The list is "what stays on the Kallax". Legacy boxes are a different shape of recommendation; I'd write that as a separate guide before pretending it competes with Spirit Island.

### Root
A great game with a teach problem I couldn't solve in 18 months on the FLGS demo table. Three of four players will play it once, learn the asymmetry hurt, and decline the rematch. Brilliant if your group commits to play five; not honest to put it in a list aimed at a mixed group.

### Terraforming Mars
Aged badly. The card pool's variance is wider than the design intends, and the production has been chasing its own tail across a dozen expansions. The 2018-era praise hasn't held up; I'd put Ark Nova or Beyond the Sun in this slot.

### Scythe
Beautiful, slow, and decided in the first 40 minutes about half the time. If you've got a group that already plays Scythe weekly, fine; for a list aimed at the typical Kallax-owner, the engagement-per-hour is too low.
